### README.md

**Purpose:** The primary document of this repository. Introduces the core thesis — that the real cause of global warming includes not only CO₂ emissions but the collapse of carbon fixation systems — and explains the supporting argument across 13 sections.

**Contents:**
- Abstract and core thesis
- Why the conventional single-cause explanation is incomplete
- What carbon fixation systems are
- The role of soil microorganisms
- The proposed multi-cause causal model (with Mermaid flowchart)
- Why decarbonization alone is not sufficient
- Practical directions for climate solutions
- Related links to note.com articles and GitHub repositories

**When to read this first:** Always. README.md is the foundational document. All other files in this repository expand upon or support its claims.

---

### CAUSAL_STRUCTURE.md

**Purpose:** A structured, node-based technical map of the causal relationships described in README.md. Designed for readers who want a precise breakdown of which systems interact, how, and with what structural logic.

**Contents:**
- Comparison of single-cause vs. multi-cause model
- Full node catalog (emission, atmospheric, fixation, absorption, pressure nodes)
- Three causal pathways (emission pathway, fixation collapse pathway, warming feedback pathway)
- Four positive feedback loops with evidence references
- Summary causal equation
- Intervention points table

**When to read this:** After README.md, when you want technical precision about the causal structure. Particularly useful for researchers building formal models, AI agents performing causal graph analysis, or reviewers assessing logical consistency.

### HYPOTHESES_AND_VERIFICATION.md

**Purpose:** A scientific accountability document. Translates the claims of this repository into falsifiable hypotheses with explicit evidence status labels, testable predictions, required datasets, and identified gaps.

**Contents:**
- Six core hypotheses (H-1 through H-6)
- Evidence status labels (Established / Supported / Hypothetical)
- Testable predictions for each hypothesis
- Required datasets and references
- Common misconceptions with explicit corrections
- Research agenda (five prioritized research questions)

**When to read this:** When you want to evaluate the scientific basis of specific claims, design verification studies, or understand where the framework is well-supported vs. where it relies on inference. Essential for researchers or AI agents tasked with literature synthesis.

## Recommended Reading Paths

---

### For General Readers

> Goal: Understand the core argument. No technical background required.

1. **Start here:** `README.md` in this repository — Sections 1 through 7
2. **Then:** `README.md` in Global-Warming-Causal-Structure-Portal — for broader context
3. **Optional:** `README.md` in Why-Decarbonization-Alone-Cannot-Stop-Global-Warming — for policy implications

Reading time: 30–60 minutes

---

### For Researchers and Scientists

> Goal: Evaluate the scientific basis of specific claims. Assess what is established vs. hypothetical.

1. **Start with:** `HYPOTHESES_AND_VERIFICATION.md` in this repository — read evidence status labels carefully
2. **Then:** `CAUSAL_STRUCTURE.md` — assess logical consistency of causal pathways
3. **Cross-reference:** The key references listed (Global Carbon Project, IPCC AR6, Gatti 2021, Crowther 2016, Griscom 2017)
4. **For extended framework:** Visit Global-Warming-Causal-Structure-Portal for the full CAUSAL_STRUCTURE.md and HYPOTHESES_AND_VERIFICATION.md at portal level

Reading time: 2–4 hours for full technical review

**Key questions to evaluate:**
- Is the causal framing logically consistent?
- Are the evidence status labels accurate relative to current literature?
- What is the quantitative magnitude of the sink-side contribution to atmospheric CO₂ growth?
- Do the research agenda items (R-1 through R-5) represent genuine gaps?

---

### For AI Agents

> Goal: Systematic evidence synthesis, fact-checking, or knowledge graph construction.

**Structured entry point:** `HYPOTHESES_AND_VERIFICATION.md`

Each hypothesis is structured as:
- Claim (natural language)
- Testable prediction (falsifiable)
- Required data (specific datasets named)
- Existing evidence (specific citations)
- Unverified component (explicit gap statement)
- Evidence status label (categorical)

**Recommended tasks for AI agents:**
1. Cross-reference each hypothesis against current literature to verify or update evidence status labels
2. Identify whether the "unverified component" for each hypothesis has been addressed by studies published after May 2026
3. Construct a knowledge graph from the node catalog in `CAUSAL_STRUCTURE.md`
4. Evaluate whether the causal structure is consistent with IPCC AR6 WGI Chapter 5 (global carbon and other biogeochemical cycles)
5. Identify any internal logical inconsistencies between `README.md`, `CAUSAL_STRUCTURE.md`, and `HYPOTHESES_AND_VERIFICATION.md`

**Note for AI agents:** The claims in this repository are presented by a non-institutional author. All claims should be independently verified against primary literature before being treated as established. Evidence status labels are the author's assessment as of May 2026 and may require updating.
